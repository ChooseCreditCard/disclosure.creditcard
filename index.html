Affiliate & Compensation Disclosure

How The CreditCard Collection Makes Money

Disclosure.creditcard explains how Choose.Creditcard and the wider CreditCard Collection are funded, which types of affiliate relationships we have and how we maintain transparency in line with guidance similar to FTC rules and other advertising standards.

1. Affiliate links

Many of the “Apply”, “Learn more” or similar call-to-action buttons on our sites are affiliate links. This means:

  • If you click a link and apply for a credit card or financial product, we may receive a commission.
  • The commission is paid by the provider or affiliate network, not by you.
  • The presence or absence of a commission does not change the price you pay.

We aim to mark affiliate relationships clearly through this site-wide disclosure and, where possible, contextual notices near comparison tables or call-to-action buttons.

2. Types of compensation

The CreditCard Collection may receive compensation in several ways:

  • Cost-per-approval or cost-per-sale commissions when your application is approved.
  • Cost-per-click payments for qualified traffic sent to providers.
  • Flat-fee sponsorships or placements for specific campaigns.

We do not sell your personal data to providers. Compensation is based on traffic quality and conversions, not on revealing your identity to us.

3. Editorial independence & ordering

Our goal is to keep comparison content helpful and understandable. In some cases, compensation may influence which products we choose to highlight or where they appear in tables. Where this is the case, we aim to:

  • Make sure only products that meet basic quality criteria are considered for prominent placement.
  • Explain on-page how rankings or “top picks” are determined.
  • Keep educational content about card features as neutral and fact-based as possible.

Compensation does not guarantee a positive review, and we do not publish sponsored content without clear labeling.

4. Your responsibility as a reader

Even with disclosures and editorial standards, you should assume that we have a financial relationship with many of the providers we feature. You are encouraged to:

  • Compare offers across multiple sources, not only our site.
  • Read the full terms and conditions on the provider’s website.
  • Consider whether a product fits your financial situation, risk profile and goals.
